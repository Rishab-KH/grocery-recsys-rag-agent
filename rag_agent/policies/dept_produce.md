# Produce Department Handling Policy

## Scope
This policy covers quality standards, organic product handling, substitution constraints, and spoilage risk management for the fresh produce department.

## Quality Standards at Pick
Pickers must visually inspect all produce items before placing them in an order. The following standards apply:

- **Whole fruit and vegetables**: No visible bruising larger than 1 cm, no mold, no soft spots indicating internal rot.
- **Leafy greens**: No yellowing, sliminess, or wilting. Bags must be sealed and undamaged.
- **Pre-cut produce**: Must be within sell-by date with a minimum of 1 day of shelf life remaining at time of pick.
- **Herbs**: Must be fully green with no black stems or visible decomposition.

Any produce item failing these standards must be left on the shelf and flagged for store replenishment. A substitute must be selected per substitution policy.

## Organic Product Handling
Organic produce carries specific handling and substitution constraints:

- Organic items may only be substituted with other certified organic items. Conventional produce is not an eligible substitute for any organic SKU, regardless of availability.
- If no organic substitute is available in the same aisle, the item is dropped from the order. Cross-aisle organic substitution is permitted only if the replacement item is from the same product category.
- Organic items must not be co-bagged with conventional items during packing to avoid cross-contamination of organic certification for the customer.

## Substitution Constraints
Produce substitutions follow the general substitution hierarchy (same aisle preferred) with the following category-specific additions:

- Stone fruit may only be substituted with other stone fruit.
- Citrus may only be substituted with other citrus.
- Root vegetables may be substituted within the root vegetable category.
- Tropical fruits are high-substitution-risk items; if no same-category substitute is available, the slot is dropped rather than substituting with an unrelated item.

Size substitutions in produce are permitted in the upward direction only (e.g., a 1 lb bag may be substituted with a 1.5 lb bag billed at weight-adjusted price). Downsizing is not permitted.

## Spoilage Risk Management
Produce is a high-spoilage-risk category. The following operational controls apply. Numeric thresholds below reflect standard operating targets; values may vary by region or carrier and should be confirmed against local SOP documentation.

- Produce picks must occur last in the fulfillment sequence, immediately before dispatch, to minimize time at ambient temperature.
- Pre-cut produce must not be held in staging for more than 20 minutes before dispatch.
- During summer months, all produce orders must include at minimum one ice pack in the delivery bag.
- Orders containing pre-cut produce assigned to a Standard window must be escalated if the window start time is more than 3 hours from pick completion.
