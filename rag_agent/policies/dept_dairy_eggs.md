# Dairy and Eggs Department Handling Policy

## Scope
This policy defines refrigeration requirements, brand and fat-content preservation standards, and substitution tolerance thresholds for the dairy and eggs department.

## Refrigeration Requirements
All dairy and egg items must remain in refrigerated storage until the point of order packing. The following handling rules apply:

- Dairy items must not be held outside refrigeration for more than 5 minutes during the pick-and-pack process.
- Items showing signs of temperature abuse — condensation inside sealed packaging, swollen containers, or any visible separation in liquid dairy — must not be fulfilled and must be flagged for removal from the shelf.
- Milk, cream, and yogurt must have a minimum of 3 days' remaining shelf life at the time of pick.
- Cheese (hard and semi-hard) requires a minimum of 7 days' remaining shelf life. Soft cheeses (ricotta, brie, fresh mozzarella) require a minimum of 3 days.

## Brand and Fat-Content Preservation
Dairy substitutions are highly sensitive to fat content and brand preference due to their impact on cooking and dietary outcomes. The following rules apply:

- **Fat content must be preserved exactly**: A 2% milk order must not be substituted with whole milk or skim milk, regardless of brand availability. Fat content is treated as a non-negotiable product attribute.
- **Plant-based alternatives**: Dairy items must not be substituted with plant-based alternatives unless the customer has explicitly indicated dietary preferences allowing plant-based substitution.
- **Brand substitution**: Permitted for commodity dairy products (butter, standard milk, basic yogurt) when the substitute shares the same fat content and format. Not permitted for specialty or premium brands without explicit customer consent.

## Eggs
- Egg cartons must be opened and inspected for cracks before fulfillment. Any carton with one or more cracked eggs must be replaced entirely.
- Egg size (small, medium, large, jumbo) must be preserved in substitution. Size substitution is not permitted.
- Free-range and certified humane eggs must only be substituted with other free-range or certified humane eggs. Conventional eggs are not eligible substitutes.

## Substitution Tolerance
The dairy and eggs department has low substitution tolerance overall. Permitted substitutions are limited to:

- Same fat content, same format, different brand (commodity dairy only).
- Same egg size, same certification tier, different brand.

Any substitution that changes fat content, egg size, or organic/free-range certification is classified as a policy violation and must be reversed before dispatch if identified during quality review.
