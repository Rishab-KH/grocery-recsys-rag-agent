# Cold-Chain Integrity Policy

## Definitions:
Cold-chain item: Any product requiring temperature-controlled handling per category standards.

## Scope
This policy defines mandatory handling, storage, and transport requirements to maintain cold-chain integrity for refrigerated and frozen products across all fulfillment operations.

## Temperature Standards
All cold-chain products must remain within the following temperature bands from pick to delivery:

| Category | Required Temp Range |
|----------|-------------------|
| Frozen | -18°C to -15°C (0°F to 5°F) |
| Refrigerated | 1°C to 4°C (34°F to 40°F) |
| Fresh produce (cold) | 4°C to 7°C (40°F to 45°F) |

Any product found outside these ranges during quality checks must be quarantined and not dispatched. Temperature excursions must be documented with time, zone, and product ID.

## Fulfillment Center Requirements
- Cold and frozen zones must be maintained at certified temperatures at all times, including during shift transitions and high-volume periods.
- Pickers must not leave refrigerated or frozen items in ambient zones for more than 3 minutes during the pick-and-pack process.
- Staging areas for cold-chain orders must be refrigerated or insulated. Ambient staging is prohibited for orders containing frozen items.

## Transport Requirements
- Delivery vehicles used for frozen orders must be equipped with active refrigeration certified to maintain -18°C.
- Refrigerated items may be transported in insulated bags with ice packs for routes under 90 minutes in ambient temperatures below 25°C.
- For routes exceeding 90 minutes or in ambient temperatures above 25°C, vehicle-level refrigeration is required for all refrigerated items.

## Substitution Restrictions for Frozen Products
Substitutions for frozen items are subject to the following restrictions:

- Substitutes must be from the same frozen category (e.g., frozen vegetables may only be substituted with other frozen vegetables).
- Substitutions that would require mixing frozen and refrigerated items in the same insulated bag are prohibited.
- No cross-temperature-zone substitutions are permitted under any workflow (e.g., substituting a frozen item with a refrigerated equivalent is not allowed).

Violations of cross-zone substitution rules must be escalated to the fulfillment supervisor before dispatch.

## Documentation and Audit
- All cold-chain shipments must have a completed cold-chain checklist attached to the order record before dispatch sign-off.
- Temperature logs from vehicle sensors must be retained for a minimum of 90 days and made available upon regulatory request.
- Any cold-chain breach resulting in customer complaint must trigger a root-cause review within 48 hours.
