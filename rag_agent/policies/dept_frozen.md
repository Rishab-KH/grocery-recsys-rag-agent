# Frozen Department Handling Policy

## Scope
This policy defines cold-chain enforcement, substitution restrictions, and delivery timing requirements specific to the frozen food department. Frozen items carry the highest cold-chain risk and the strictest substitution constraints of any department.

## Cold-Chain Enforcement
Frozen items are subject to the full cold-chain requirements defined in `cold_chain.md`, with the following department-specific additions:

- Frozen items must be picked last among frozen SKUs in the fulfillment sequence and packed immediately into insulated packaging.
- Frozen items must never be staged in ambient or refrigerated zones, even temporarily. Frozen staging zones only.
- If a fulfillment center's frozen staging zone is at capacity, frozen orders must be queued and picked only when dispatch is imminent (within 10 minutes of driver pickup).
- Any frozen item with packaging damage (torn bag, punctured box, compromised seal) must not be fulfilled regardless of sell-by date.

## Substitution Rules
Frozen substitutions are the most restricted of any department:

- Substitutions are permitted only within the same frozen subcategory (e.g., frozen vegetables for frozen vegetables, frozen meals for frozen meals, frozen fruit for frozen fruit).
- Brand substitution is permitted for commodity frozen items (plain frozen vegetables, basic frozen fruit) but not for branded frozen meals, specialty items, or dietary-specific products.
- No cross-subcategory substitutions are permitted (e.g., frozen pizza may not be substituted with a frozen meal, frozen berries may not be substituted with frozen vegetables).
- No temperature-zone crossovers: a frozen item may never be substituted with a refrigerated or ambient equivalent under any circumstances.

Organic frozen items follow the same organic preservation rule as produce: organic may only be substituted with certified organic.

## Delivery Timing Impact
Frozen orders have strict delivery window requirements:

- Frozen items may not be assigned to Extended delivery windows (4+ hours).
- For Standard windows (2–4 hours), active refrigeration in the delivery vehicle is mandatory.
- Routes containing frozen items must be sequenced to complete frozen deliveries before non-frozen stops wherever operationally feasible.
- If a frozen order's delivery window is breached by more than 30 minutes due to route delay, a spoilage review must be completed and a pre-authorized credit issued.

## Customer Communication
If frozen items are substituted or dropped:

- Customer must be notified before dispatch with the original SKU, outcome, and reason code.
- Any temperature breach during transport that may have affected frozen item integrity must be disclosed to the customer at delivery, and a full refund pre-authorized for affected items.
